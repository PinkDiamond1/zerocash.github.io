Zerocash

Zerocash is a new privacy-preserving protocol for digital currency designed to sit on top of Bitcoin, or similar currencies. The Zerocash protocol fixes a major weakness in Bitcoin that deprives users of the privacy guarantees we take for granted in using credit cards and cash: that our neighbors, friends, and everyone else doesn't see what we bought or for how much.

The Zerocash protocol extends Bitcoin and enables users to pay one another directly, via payment transactions that reveal neither the origin, destination, or amount of the payment. This is a marked improvement on the protocols underlying Bitcoin and similar decentralized digital currency systems, where every transaction in the system is public to the whole world.

Zerocash improves on an earlier protocol, Zerocoin, developed by some of the same authors, both in functionality (Zerocoin only hides a payment's origin, but not the amount or destination) and in efficiency (Zerocash transactions are less than 1KB and take less than 6ms to verify).

How does Zerocash work?

The Zerocash protocol is designed to extend the Bitcoin protocol and software, by adding efficient transaction privacy as an overlay to Bitcoin's existing features. Where Zerocash differs from Bitcoin is in the way that transactions are formulated. Standard Bitcoin transactions consist of a source address, destination address and transaction amount. These transactions are stored on a public ledger called the "block chain" and can be viewed by any party. Zerocash transactions, on the other hand, contain no public information about the source, destination or amount of any transaction, instead using a Zero-knowledge proof of correctness. Users can covert bitcoins into zerocoins, send zerocoins to other users, and split or merge zerocoins they own in any way that preserves the total value. Users may also convert zerocoins back into bitcoins, though in principle this is not necessary: all transactions can be made in terms of zerocoins.
